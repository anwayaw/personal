Hello!

I'm Matt, a human-centered designer and design technologist currently in Tokyo, Japan. With roots in rural Pennsylvania near Pittsburgh, I gained an interest in computers and art from a young age. This curiosity led me from humble beginnings to a fulfilling career in design, spanning over 16 years of experience with startups and agencies across Pittsburgh, New York City, and Silicon Valley.

My Journey

While I was interested in computers and art, I felt computer science was lacking in creativity, and that art was lacking in structure and rigor. Discovering Human-Computer Interaction combined my passions. I studied Information Sciences and Technology at Penn State, laying the foundation for my career. A pivotal moment was studying at Carnegie Mellon's Human-Computer Interaction Institute, where I earned my M.S. and expanded my understanding of design and research methods.

Since then, I've had the pleasure of working with various design agencies, including Moment Design and Sequence, as well as product teams at Location Labs and Salesforce. Currently, I'm a Staff Experience Designer at Included Health, where I continue to push the boundaries of what's possible in human-centered design in the healthcare space.

Design Philosophy

My approach to design is guided by three core principles:

1. "Just enough" design

I believe that great design often lies in what you choose to leave out. The goal is to create intuitive experiences that get out of the user's way, allowing them to achieve their goals without unnecessary complexity or gimmicks.

2. Integrated design and engineering

The best products come from teams where tight multidisciplinary collaboration is the norm. When ideas flow freely between designers, engineers, and everyone in between, magic happens. I try to use my knowledge of design, strategy, and development help bridge these worlds effectively.

3. Always adapting

In our rapidly evolving technological landscape, continual adaptation is key. I embrace new paradigms and technologies like AI with enthusiasm, believing that staying current not only improves our work but also turbo charges creative exploration and innovation.
